Posted on 2020-03-31 In learning

Group33

  • counterbalance
  • undermine
  • undertake
  • underlie
  • underline
  • corroborate
  • conclude
  • collaborate
  • undervalue
  • conjecture

例句：

Augustus thought that increased luxury had undermined traditional Roman frugality(节俭) and led to a loosening of morals.

Jobs traditionally done by women are poorly paid and undervalued.

And if we insist that political obligations must be undertaken voluntarily, this is a risk we always run.

Recent research shows how the stereotyping(刻板印象) that underlies prejudice is a by-product of our thinking——our ways of simplifying the world.

Lee uses the camera inventively to underline social conflicts, as in his radical close-ups(特写) of faces caught in bitter(苦的，激烈的), heated disputes.

Riskier investments tend to be counterbalanced by high rewards.

What is Cole and Ohanian’s alternative explanation for the length of the recovery during the Great Depression? They conjecture that regulation was the culprit(罪魁祸首).

The major findings of the earlier work were corroborated, but some further developments were found.

Despite a general economic recovery beginning in the mid-1980s, however, job insecurity continued to grow. The study concluded that job insecurity had reached its highest point since the Second World War.

Designers also collaborated with architects, who became more involved in interior decoration.

单词释义：

undermine: to weaken, injure, or impair, often by degrees or imperceptibly

破坏

undervalue: to assign too low a value to; to have too little regard or esteem for

低估…的价值；看轻

undertake: to deliberately begin to do (something); to pledge or commit oneself (to do something)

承担，保证

underlie: to be the support or basis of

成为…的基础

underline: to emphasize

强调

counterbalance: to act as a counteracting force, influence, or weight to; to oppose with an equal force

使平衡，抵消

conjecture: to judge or conclude by conjecture; to make a conjecture

推测，猜想

corroborate: to strengthen or support with other evidence

证实

conclude: to arrive at (a conclusion, judgment, or opinion) by the process of reasoning

推断，断定，决定

collaborate: to work together, especially in a joint intellectual effort

合作，勾结
