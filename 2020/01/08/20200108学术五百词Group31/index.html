学术五百词Group31

发表于2020-01-08|更新于2020-03-08|learning

Group31

  • suppress
  • subordinate
  • subdivide
  • superimpose
  • supersede
  • survey
  • sustain
  • usurp
  • subtract
  • substantiate

例句：

The women were subordinated to men.

If we define meaning as helping people in faraway places, we implicitly subtract meaning from the actual work we are doing.

These numbers exist for any reason other than to substantiate a fragile argument.

To reorganize local government, Catherine divided Russia into fifty provinces, each of which was in turn subdivided into districts ruled by officials chosen by the nobles.

A photo of a cup of cappuccino had been superimposed on a picture of Venice.

By the mid-fifteenth century, the French nobility(贵族) was badly depleted, and those knights who survived the war found themselves “outdated”. In France, feudal allegiances(封建效忠) were soon superseded by systems of national conscription(全国征兵).

Some historians have argued that the desire to suppress internal disorder may have encouraged some leaders to take the plunge into war in 1914.

Scholars did not begin to survey Buddhist(佛教的) music until the early twentieth century.

As precipitation in a warming and wetter world falls more as rain and less as snow, the likely result will be rainy season floods and less dry season snow and ice melt to sustain rivers.

Industrialism began to usurp the production and distribution functions of the family.

单词释义：

subordinate: to put in a lower or inferior rank or calss; to make subservient
从属，使服从

subtract: to take away
减去，扣掉

substantiate: to prove the truth of or support with proof or evidence
证实，使实体化

subdivide: to form into subdivisions
细分

superimpose: to lay or place (something) on or over something else
添加，叠加

supersede: to take the place of
取代，代替

suppress: to put an end to forcibly; to curtail or prohibit the activities of; to keep from being revealed
抑制，镇压

survey: to look over the parts, features, or contents of; to determine the boundaries, area, or elevations by means of measuring angles and distances, using the techniques of geometry and trigonometry
调查，测量，勘探

sustain: to keep in existence; to supply with necessities or nourishment
维持

usurp: to seize and hold (the power or rights of another, for example) by force or without legal authority
篡夺
