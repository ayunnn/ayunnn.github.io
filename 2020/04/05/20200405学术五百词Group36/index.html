Group36

  • postulate
  • permit
  • obviate
  • originate
  • participate
  • mitigate
  • mediate
  • necessitate
  • motivate
  • negate

These peacemaking principles—based partly on laboratory experiments, partly on practical experience—have helped mediate both international and industrial conflicts.

Moreover, such data will mitigate or obviate the problems we discussed earlier related to poor measurement of events and event time.

Attempts to explain the Black Death and mitigate its harshness led to extreme sorts of behavior.

单词释义：

mitigate: to make less severe or intense

减轻，缓和

mediate: to resolve or settle (differences) by working with all the conflicting parties; to work with two or more disputants in order to bring about an agreement, settlement, or compromise

调停

motivate: to provide with an incentive or a reason for doing something; to cause to be enthusiastic

刺激，激发…的积极性

negate: to make ineffective or invalid; to make negative

否定

necessitate: to make necessary or unavoidable; to require or compel

使成为必须

obviate: to keep from happening or render unnecessary

排除，避免

originate: to come into being

发源，发生

participate: to be active or involved in something

参与，参加

postulate: to assume or assert the truth, reality, or necessity of, especially as a basis of an argument; to propose as a hypothesis or explanation

假定

permit: to allow the doing of (something); to afford opportunity or possibility for

许可，允许

Note

power plant: 发电厂
