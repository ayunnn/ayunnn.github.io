学术五百词Group40

发表于 2020-04-10 分类于 learning Valine：

Group40

  • align
  • argue
  • contrast
  • coincide
  • acquire
  • interconnect
  • outweigh
  • occur
  • observe
  • acquiesce

例句：

Theosophists(学者) claimed that all creation is part of one eternal life, a Radical Unity, and that everything is therefore mystically interconnected.

The rising wealth of a small segment of the population came to contrast with widespread poverty and old aristocratic privilege.

The Israelites(以色列人) believed that during the Exodus from Egypt, when Moses supposedly led his people out of bondage(奴役) into the “promised land,” a special event occurred that determined the Jewish experience for all time.

By the time the king was executed(处死), most of Europe——an informal coalition(联合) of Austria, Prussia, Spain, Portugal, Britain, the Dutch Republic, and Russia——had aligned against France, and by late spring, some members of the coalition were poised to invade France in an effort to destroy the revolutionaries and reestablish the old regime.

Gladiatorial(争论的) games increased dramatically in the Early Empire, and upper-class women acquired greater independence.

The exhibition coincides with the 50th anniversary of his birthday.

The benefits of the scheme outweigh the disadvantages.

There is no single viewpoint from which to observe the mountains, trees, waters, and human habitations.

The researchers argued for banning the drug and put forward a well-argued case.

The cardinal(红衣主教) acquiesces, and Portuguese troops are sent to take over the missions.

单词释义：

interconnect: to be connected with each other

互相联系

contrast: to show differences when compared

对比，对照

coincide: to happen at the same time or during the same period

一致，符合

outweigh: to be more significant than

比…重要

occur: to take place; to be found to exist or appear

发生，出现

observe: to be or become aware of; to watch attentively

观察，遵守

align: to ally (oneself, for example) with one side of an argument or cause

使结盟，对齐

argue: to give evidence of; to engage in a quarrel; to put forth reasons for or against something

争论，辩论

acquire: to get by one’s own efforts; to gain through experience; to gain possession of

获得，取得

acquiesce: to consent or comply passively or without protest

默许，勉强同意
