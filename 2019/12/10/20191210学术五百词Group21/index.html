学术五百词Group21

发表于2019-12-10|更新于2020-03-08|learning

Group21

  • exhibit
  • exert
  • export
  • exemplify
  • examine
  • exceed
  • expound
  • experience
  • exploit
  • expropriate

例句：

Thus, chronically(长期的) self-conscious people are especially likely to drink following a failure. If recovering from alcoholism, they are more likely than those low in self-consciousness to relapse when they again experience stress or failure.

Not only are migrants more at risk of poverty, but they also face a higher risk of being exploited at work.

The radicals(激进分子) want to carry on with and deepen the Islamic revolution; they also believe that the revolution should be actively exported to other Islamic countries.

Social pressure, public opinion, fear of a poor reputation, even gossip, can all exert their effects on individual behavior.

Two of the leading feminists(女权主义者) work on women and organizations exemplified the split between liberal and radical feminist perspectives.

Before writing the Politics, he (Aristotle) examined the constitutions(宪法) of more than 150 Greek citystates(城邦).

A Greek author of the Roman period, Plutarch wrote Parallel Lives to show that ancient Greece matched or exceeded Rome in its great leaders.

Passionately affirming the Enlightenment’s (启蒙运动) confidence in reason and science, Condorcet expounded a theory of continuous and indefinite human improvement. He pointed toward a future golden age, characterized by the triumph of reason and freedom.

Contrary to the presumption(假设) that most people suffer from low self-esteem or feelings of inferiority(自卑), researchers consistently find that most people exhibit a self-serving bias. In experiments and everyday life, we often take credit for our successes while blaming failures on the situation.

The libertarian(自由意志主义者), uniquely, holds that it is just as wrong for governments to take people’s money or expropriate their lands as for privately acting individuals or gangs to do so.

单词释义：

export: to send or transport(a commodity, for example) abroad, especially for trade or sale; to cause the spread of (an idea, for example) in another part of the world
输出，出口

exemplify: to illustrate by example; to serve as an example of
例证，示例

examine: to study or analyze
检查，调查

exceed: to be greater than, as in number or degree; to go beyond the limits of; to be better than or superior to
超过，胜过

expound: to explain in detail
详述，阐述

exhibit: to present in a public exhibition or contest; to give evidence or an instance of
展览，展示

exert: to put to use or effect; to bring to bear
运用，发挥

exploit: to make use of selfishly or unethically
开发，开拓，剥削

expropriate: to take (a property) for public use
没收，征用

Notes

今天从文献中学到的一种表达，用在结论里：
While these effects are undoubtedly important, out study further revealed that… These findings demonstrate that…

虽然现有的文献提出的影响因素很重要，我们的研究进一步揭示了…
