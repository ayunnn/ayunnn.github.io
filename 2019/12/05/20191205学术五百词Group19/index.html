Group19

  • mobilize
  • organize
  • recognize
  • moderate
  • restrict
  • orient
  • modernize
  • minimize
  • modify
  • model

例句：

Despite a recent rush to modernize and become a world power, Russia in some respects remained trapped in the Middle Ages.

Mining communities initially organized to protest against the policy, but, when this eventually failed, many individual miners retrained to find work in other industries.

Insisting on military service from the allies in the Roman Confederation, Rome essentially mobilized the entire military manpower of all Italy for its wars.

In Freud’s view, civilization is the product of the ego’s effort to modify the primal urges of the id.
（ego:自我 id:本我）

The plebeians(平民) hoped that they could restrict the arbitrary power of the patrician(贵族的) magistrates(行政长官), who alone had access to the laws.

Its four sides are almost precisely oriented to the four points of the compass.

With this population size, humans have already overshot the Earth’s carrying capacity, so consumption must also moderate.

One way people minimize dissonance, Festinger believed, is through selective exposure to agreeable information.

Prehistoric artists also carved and modeled sculptures in a variety of materials.

The Christians did not recognize other gods and therefore abstained from public festivals honoring these divinities(神).

单词释义：

moderate: to cause to be less extreme
节制，减轻，缓和
adj. 温和的

modernize: to make modern in appearance, style, or character; to accept or adopt modern ways
现代化

modify: to change in form or chatacter; to make less extreme, severe, or strong
修改

model: to plan, construct, or fashion in imitation of a model; to form (clay, for example) into a shape
模拟，塑造，模仿

mobilize: to assemble, prepare, or put into active service; to assemble, marshal, or coordinate for a purpose
动员，组织

recognize: to know to be something that has been perceiced before; to know or identify from past experience or knowledge
认出，确认，承认

orient: to align or position in a particular direction or in a particular relation to the points of the compass; to determine the bearings of (oneself); to provide with a primary purpose or focus of attention
朝向，确定方位
