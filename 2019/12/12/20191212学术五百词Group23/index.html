Group23

例句：

If the guidance of education is not returned to the priests, and if science is not uniformly relegated to a subordinate(从属的) rank, incalculable evils await us.

In such a situation life is truly miserable, not only racked by fear, but lacking material comforts and sources of well-being. As no one can be sure of retaining any possessions, few will plant or cultivate, or engage in any long-term enterprise or plan.

And most participants are from WEIRD (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, and Democratic) cultures that represent but 12 percent of humanity.

They (The masses) feel that they are participants in a mighty movement that is destined to regenerate the German nation and initiate a new historical age.

The Neolithic(新石器时代) period also marked the beginning of the use of metals. The first to be used was copper, which was easily fashioned into tools and weapons. Copper implements lasted longer than those of stone and flint, and they could be recast and reshaped if broken.

The Chinese have long regarded the era of the Han as their classical age, and to this day refer to themselves as the “children of the Han”.

单词释义：

relegate: to consign to an inferior or obscure place, rank, category, or condition
贬职，降低

regenerate: to form, construct, or create anew; to give new life or energy to
再生，革新

recast: to mold again; to set down or present(ideas, for example) in a new or different arrangement
重铸

refer: to relate or pertain; to serve as a descriptor or have as a denotation
参考，涉及，提到

refute: to prove to be false or erroneous; to deny the accuracy or truth of
反驳，驳倒

retard: to cause to move or proceed slowly
延迟，减慢

represent: to have as a meaning, suggestion, or association; to serve as a delegate or agent for
代表，象征

regard: to think of or consider in a particular way
注意，考虑，重视

retain: to continue to have as a feature or aspect
保持

obtain: to succeed in gaining possession of as the result of planning or endeavor
获得

Notes

but

今天才知道，but居然有多种词性。
adv. 表示仅仅，只有，相当于only。
I don’t think we’ll manage it. Still, we can but try.

n. 借口，推辞
‘Let us have no buts,’ he said firmly. ‘You are coming.’
“别找借口了。”他坚定地说，“你得来。”
