Group24

  • standardize
  • synthesize
  • stimulate
  • strengthen
  • structure
  • situate
  • suggest

例句：

Evidence indicates that artists and workshops standardized their production techniques, subcontracted specific elements of projects, and specialized in particular forms or subjects.

Dürer’s painting technique owes much to the Flemish masters but making copies of Italian works taught him many of the lessons of the Italian Renaissance. He was able to synthesize these traditions in his paintings and prints.

Napoleon believed in using the resources of government to stimulate the national economy and took many steps to expand industrial growth.

To strengthen the sculptures’ message, the designer included narrative(叙事的) elements in the pediment as well.

The most important was the centuriated assembly, essentially the Roman army functioning in its political role. Organized by classes based on wealth, it was structured in such a way that the wealthiest citizens always had a majority.

Eighteen centuries before Copernicus, the Alexandrian astronomer Aristarchus (310–230 b.c.) said that the sun was the center of the universe, that the planets revolved around it, and that the stars were situated at great distances from the earth.

Liberals believed that education was important to personal and social improvement and sought in Catholic countries to supplant Catholic education with moral and civic training based on secular(世俗的) values.

So many bodies were brought to the churches every day that the consecrated(神圣的) ground did not suffice to hold them.

Linguistic clues can suggest past contacts between cultures.

单词释义：
standardize: to cause to conform to a standard
使标准化

synthesize: to combine so as to form a new, complex product
合成，综合

stimulate: to rouse to action or increased activity
刺激，鼓舞

strengthen: to make strong or increase the strength of
加强

structure: to give form or arrangement to
组织，构成

situate: to place in a certain spot or position; to place in a given context
使位于

suggest: to offer for consideration or action; to bring or call to mind by logic or association
提议，建议

supplant: to take the place of or substitute for (another)
代替

suffice: to meet present needs or requirements; to be equal to a specified task
使满足，足够
