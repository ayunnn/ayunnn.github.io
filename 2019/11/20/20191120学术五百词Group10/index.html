学术五百词Group10

发表于2019-11-20|更新于2020-03-08|learning

Group10

  • design
  • define
  • deviate
  • devalue
  • delimit
  • detract
  • redefine
  • deconstruct
  • decode
  • diffuse

例句：

No society can be divided up in a simple way between those who deviate from norms and those who conform to them.

Philosophy and wisdom define our place in the universe and give our lives meaning.

Since Luther downplayed the role of good works in salvation, the sacraments also had to be redefined.

Language theorists suggested that one must “deconstruct“ or “take apart” discourse in order to “unmask” its many meanings.

In Paris, the cathedral of Notre-Dame was designated a “temple of reason”.
Some claimed that the emperor had been designated by his father.

Among married couples, too, those who worry that their partner doesn’t love and accept them interpret slight hurts as rejections, which motivates them to devalue the partner and distance themselves.

If you decode a message that has been written or spoken in a code, you change it into ordinary language.

One mistake is not going to detract from your achievement.

To determine whether a science has its value to exists, it is essential to delimit the range of its research objects.

Their ideas diffused quickly across Europe.

单词释义：

deviate: to cause to turn aside or differ; to turn aside from a course or way
脱离，使偏离

delimit: to establish the limits or boundaries of
划界，定界限

detract: to draw or take away; to reduce the value, importance, or quality of something
贬低，转移

devalue: to lessen or cancel the value of
使贬值

decode: to extract the underlying meaning from
解码，译码

diffuse: to cause to spread out freely; to make known to or cause to be used by large numbers of people
扩散，传播

designate: to indicate or specify; to give a name or title to; to select and set aside for a duty, office, or purpose
指定，指派

define: to state the precise meaning of (a word or sense of a word, for example); to describe the nature or basic qualities of; to specify distinctly
定义，使明确，规定

redefine: to define (as a concept) again
重新定义

deconstruct: to break down into component; to analyze (a literary text, for example) by deconstruction or in such a way as to expose its underlying assumptions or implicit ideological stance; to adapt (a genre, style, or form) in a way that isolates familiar elements from their usual context in order to imply an ironic comment on the unspoken values of the original
解构，拆解
